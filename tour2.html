Pick one of places as your destination

Chhota Imambara is a historic monument in Lucknow, India, built in the 19th century, known for its beautiful architecture and religious significance.

Ajmer, Rajasthan, is culturally rich with Taragarh Fort, Ana Sagar Lake, Mayo College, and annual Pushkar Camel Fair as notable attractions.

Mawsynram, a village in northeastern India, is known as the wettest place on Earth, receiving an annual rainfall of approximately 467 inches, creating lush green landscapes and unique ecosystems.

Amritsar, in India's Punjab state, is renowned for its cultural and spiritual significance. The city is home to the Golden Temple, a Sikh pilgrimage site, and boasts vibrant traditions and cuisine.

Hampi, located in Karnataka, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its magnificent ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, showcasing ancient temples, rock-cut structures, and a rich historical legacy.

Rishikesh, nestled in the Himalayan foothills, is a spiritual and adventure hub in India. It's famous for yoga, meditation, the Ganges River, and thrilling activities like rafting and trekking.

Jaisalmer, known as the "Golden City," is a desert town in Rajasthan, India. It's celebrated for its stunning sandstone architecture, desert landscapes, camel safaris, and the Jaisalmer Fort

Lohagad Fort, in Lonavala, Maharashtra, India, is a historic hill fort with impressive architecture and panoramic views. It's a popular trekking destination and holds historical significance.

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, India, is a charming hill station. It's famous for its colonial-era architecture, cool climate, lush landscapes, and panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Chennai, on India's southeastern coast, is a vibrant metropolis. It's known for its rich culture, Marina Beach, historical temples, IT sector, and delicious South Indian cuisine.
